I would probably pursue art full-time if it payed the bills - at least until I missed science (I'm a flip-flopper!). I'm saddened that I had to put artsy outlets mostly to rest in 2013 to adequately do my full-time job. Since I returned to do a PhD in 2018, though, I've had a little more time to revisit art. If I could, I would probably choose to prioritise immersive electronic installation art or film. What I know for sure is artistically I'm always inspired by at least one of two themes: comedy and science.

- What excites me about art and science: the potential to embed scientifically derived knowledge into inspirational art pieces.
- What excites me about comedy: making people laugh through atypical art / performances.

I've written, produced, directed, or acted in several short films, some which were accepted and screened at film festivals. Here is one of the first comedic shorts I did. My collaborators and I were super jazzed to once have a weird comedy short written and directed by Andrew Gormley be featured by the JASH (Sarah Silverman, Michael Cera, Tim and Eric, and Reggie Watts) 'Buh' comedy channel. I've also written and performed comedy, and had a couple of independent exhibits in the vein of new media / installation art.

I think it's important to note - especially for other amateur artists - that some of what I've done I consider a total and embarassing failure that I now laugh at and that's ok. I mostly attribute these failures to (1) tools I haven't fully developed (including patience) to adequately evaluate and refine an idea before executing and (2) a lack of time. I'm getting better, though, and I'm very grateful to the CFAT New Media Scholarship which afforded me about 6 months of dedicated focus and a mentor, Stephen Kelly. This support was instrumental in getting my first public exhibition at the Nocturne Art at Night Festival and a commissioned work for the Atlantic Canada Ice Awards.

And, since you asked, I also strongly believe that everyone is an 'artist' (i.e. has a need to do creative work), it's just relatively privileged people like me that are given opportunities to explore that side of themselves.

Stay tuned for some Augmented Reality which I'm working on with my friend and long-time collaborator, Sebastian Harder
